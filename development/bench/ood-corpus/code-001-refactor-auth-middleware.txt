A team's legacy Node.js Express middleware handles authentication via three
separate functions executing in sequence on every protected route:
`authenticate(req, res, next)`, `loadUserSession(req, res, next)`, and
`enforceRateLimit(req, res, next)`. The three share repeated work (parsing
the Authorization header, querying Redis for session data) and have grown
to ~600 lines with overlapping responsibilities.

Propose a refactored design that:
- Unifies the three functions into ONE coherent auth middleware
- Eliminates the repeated work (Redis lookup happens once)
- Preserves backward compatibility (existing route handlers must not break)
- Adds testability (each step independently mockable)
- Documents the new contract for downstream route handlers

Return:
- `:refactored-design` — a structured outline of the new module shape
- `:migration-plan` — ordered steps to apply the refactor without downtime
- `:risks` — what could break, what would surface as a test failure
- `:rationale` — 2-3 paragraphs explaining the design decisions
