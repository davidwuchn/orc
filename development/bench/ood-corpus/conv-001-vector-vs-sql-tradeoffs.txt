A small B2B SaaS team is debating whether to add a vector database (e.g.,
pgvector extension on their existing Postgres, OR a dedicated Weaviate/
Qdrant deployment) to power a "find similar customer-support tickets"
feature, OR whether to handle it with a Postgres LIKE + Elasticsearch
hybrid.

The team has:
- 12 engineers (4 backend, 4 frontend, 2 SRE, 2 mobile)
- ~3M support tickets in Postgres today
- Strong Postgres operational expertise
- No prior vector-DB experience
- 6-week timeline to ship the feature
- Existing Elasticsearch cluster (3-node, used for full-text search on tickets)

Explain the tradeoffs across these three options with a clear recommendation
for THIS team given THEIR context (not in the abstract). Include:
- `:option-summaries` — what each option actually does at a 200-foot level
- `:fit-to-team-constraints` — how each option lands against the team's
  current expertise, infrastructure, and timeline
- `:operational-cost-comparison` — what each costs to run + maintain over 1
  year and 3 years
- `:quality-comparison` — relevance, latency, recall expectations
- `:recommendation` — pick ONE option + explain why for THIS team
- `:caveats` — what conditions would change the recommendation
