A consumer-app startup needs an architecture recommendation for their next
6-month build:

- Product: real-time multiplayer trivia game, expected scale 50K concurrent
  users at launch, 500K within 6 months
- Latency budget: question-broadcast to all participants in a match must
  arrive within 200 ms p99
- Team: 6 engineers, 2 of whom have GraphQL experience, none with WebRTC
- Existing infra: AWS, terraform-managed, RDS Postgres for user data,
  Cloudflare on the edge
- Cost budget: $3K/mo at 50K users, scaling sublinearly thereafter

Recommend an architecture with specific tech choices for:
- Real-time message transport (WebSocket vs Server-Sent Events vs polling)
- Game-state authority (server-authoritative vs hybrid)
- Match orchestration (single-process matchmaker vs distributed)
- Data tier (Postgres alone vs Postgres + Redis vs separate write/read)
- Observability stack

For EACH choice include:
- The decision
- Why it fits the constraints
- What FAILURE MODE it creates and how it's mitigated
- A plausibility test the team can run in week 1 to validate the choice
  before sinking implementation effort
