A junior engineer on your team has been stuck for two days on a bug: a
checkout flow occasionally charges a customer twice. They've added logging,
re-read the Stripe docs, and stared at the code. They're frustrated and
considering asking you to "just take a look at it."

Instead of solving the bug yourself, walk the junior through a debugging
strategy they can apply NEXT TIME they hit a similar wall. Make it
actionable — explain the principles AND the specific steps you'd take if
you were them on THIS bug.

Cover:
- How to break the problem space into testable hypotheses
- How to construct minimum-reproducible cases
- When to add logging vs. attach a debugger vs. read the upstream library
  source
- How to recognize you're at a wall vs. genuinely making progress
- When to escalate (and how to frame the escalation so the senior can help
  efficiently)
- The specific FIRST 2-3 steps you'd take TONIGHT on this checkout-double-
  charge bug

Tone: warm, concrete, no condescension. The junior is smart and motivated;
they just haven't built the troubleshooting reflexes yet.
