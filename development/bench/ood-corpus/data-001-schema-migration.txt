A production Postgres table `orders` (28M rows, ~12 GB, ~5K inserts/min,
~12K reads/min) needs the column `customer_email` migrated from
`varchar(255) NULL` to `varchar(255) NOT NULL`. Today ~3% of rows have
NULL (legacy guest checkouts). The app writes new orders with a non-NULL
email but doesn't enforce.

Plan a zero-downtime migration. Cover:
- The ordered DDL + DML steps (with rollback at each)
- The data backfill strategy for the 840K NULL rows (where do they get
  emails from? customer_id can join to users.email, but ~5% of orders
  are guest checkouts with NO matching user record)
- The application-side guards that prevent new NULL writes during the
  migration window
- How to detect partial-migration state (rows backfilled but constraint
  not yet applied)
- The locking implications of `ALTER TABLE ... SET NOT NULL` on a
  28M-row table and how to avoid table-wide locks
- Verification checks at each step
- Total estimated migration window and the rollback plan if a step fails
  past the point of safe rollback

Output a stepwise migration runbook the on-call engineer could follow
without further guidance.
