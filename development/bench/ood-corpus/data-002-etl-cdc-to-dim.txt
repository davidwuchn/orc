A company has 5 source databases (Postgres for transactional, MySQL for
legacy ERP, MongoDB for product catalog, Snowflake for warehoused
analytics, Salesforce for CRM). They need to build a dimensional model
(star schema) for executive dashboards.

CDC streams from the 5 sources are available via Debezium → Kafka.
Update frequencies vary: transactional ~1K events/min, ERP ~10/min,
catalog ~5/min, Snowflake batch every 4 hours, Salesforce ~200/min.

Design the ETL pipeline:
- Identify the fact tables and dimension tables (a "customer orders by
  product category" star at minimum)
- Choose an SCD strategy per dimension (Type 1 vs Type 2 vs Type 6) with
  reasoning grounded in WHO will query the data + what questions they
  ask
- Define the join logic to reconcile customer IDs across sources
  (Salesforce uses external_id, transactional uses customer_id,
  legacy ERP uses cust_no — and they aren't always perfectly matched)
- Handle late-arriving facts (Snowflake batch lags transactional by up to
  4 hours)
- Define the freshness SLA for dashboards (real-time? 15-min? hourly?)
  and justify with consumer needs

Return:
- `:fact-tables` + `:dimension-tables` (name, grain, columns, SCD type)
- `:ingestion-flow` (per-source path through Kafka → transform → load)
- `:reconciliation-strategy` for customer-ID conflicts
- `:freshness-sla` + the architectural choices that achieve it
- `:open-questions` — things you'd need to ask the business before finalizing
