You need to design a streaming aggregation that computes "items sold per
SKU per 5-minute window" with the following properties:

- Event source: 50K events/second from POS terminals across 800 stores
- Each event is `{:store-id :sku :quantity :sold-at :received-at}` —
  `:received-at` may be MINUTES later than `:sold-at` for offline-mode
  terminals that batch-upload when reconnected
- Some events arrive up to 6 hours late (worst case)
- Downstream consumers (executive dashboard) need <2-min freshness for
  CURRENT windows
- A separate consumer (inventory forecasting) needs ACCURATE final
  counts even if it costs 6+ hours
- Compute infrastructure: Kafka + Flink (the team has 2 engineers
  comfortable with both)

Design the aggregation pipeline with:
- The choice of WATERMARK strategy and what it costs
- The choice of WINDOW type (tumbling, sliding, session) and why
- The TWO output streams (one for the fast-freshness dashboard, one for
  the accurate-final-count forecaster) and how they share computation
- Late-data policy: what gets dropped vs upserted into the late-arrival
  output stream
- The reconciliation strategy for cases where the dashboard had stale
  data that's now corrected (UI considerations matter here, not just
  data-plane considerations)

Output a design doc with sections covering each of the above.
