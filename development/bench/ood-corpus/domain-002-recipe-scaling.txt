A small restaurant has a chef-tested recipe for ratatouille that yields
6 plates from these ingredients + technique:

Ingredients:
- 2 medium eggplants, diced 1 cm
- 3 zucchini, diced 1 cm
- 2 red bell peppers, diced 1 cm
- 4 plum tomatoes, peeled + diced
- 1 large onion, finely chopped
- 4 garlic cloves, minced
- 1/2 cup olive oil
- 2 tsp salt, 1 tsp ground black pepper
- 1 tbsp dried herbes de Provence
- Fresh basil for garnish

Technique:
- Sweat onions and garlic in olive oil 5 min
- Add peppers, cook 5 min
- Add eggplant + zucchini, cook 10 min with occasional stirring
- Add tomatoes + seasoning, simmer covered 25 min
- Uncover, reduce 10 min

Scale this recipe to feed 60 plates for a catering event. NOT a simple
10x multiplication — provide a scaled recipe that accounts for:
- Knife work + dicing time at scale (suggest a workflow + station setup)
- Cookware capacity (a 12" pan can hold ~6 plates of vegetables; what
  changes with scaling?)
- Heat distribution in larger pans / pots (timing adjustments)
- Seasoning at scale (salt doesn't scale linearly with vegetable volume)
- Texture preservation (over-stirring with larger batches mashes the
  vegetables)
- Service timing (how far in advance can this be cooked + held without
  texture loss)
- Cost estimate at typical restaurant supplier pricing
