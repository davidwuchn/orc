Reduce the second movement (Andante) of Mozart's Symphony No. 40 in G
minor (K. 550) — originally scored for full orchestra (strings + winds) —
to a string quartet arrangement (2 violins, viola, cello).

You don't have the score in front of you; work from your knowledge of
the piece. Produce:
- `:voice-assignments` — for each thematic moment of the movement, which
  quartet instrument takes the lead melody, which provide harmonic
  texture, and which fills rhythmic support
- `:range-considerations` — call out any passage that requires a
  transposition or octave shift to fit a quartet voice's range
  (e.g., violin 1 going below G3, viola going above C6, cello stretching
  for the original double-bass low end)
- `:harmonic-decisions` — when the original has 7-8 simultaneous voices
  but the quartet has 4, which voices do you keep + which do you omit,
  and what's the principle (root + 3rd + 5th + 7th preserves harmonic
  identity; doubled octaves drop first)
- `:dynamics-and-articulation` — what the quartet must do that the
  orchestra didn't (more aggressive bowing for sustained chords, etc.)
  to preserve the emotional arc
- `:loss-and-gain` — what's IRREDUCIBLY lost moving from orchestra to
  quartet (sheer mass of sound) and what the quartet GAINS (intimacy,
  interplay)

Output as a structured arrangement document a chamber-music coach could
use to read-through with their quartet.
