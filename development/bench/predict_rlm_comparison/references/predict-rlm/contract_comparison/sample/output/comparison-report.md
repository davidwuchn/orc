### 4.2 Appendix B – Calculation of Indexed Contract Price

**Identical structure and formula in both versions**

Key elements:

- **First Price Period**:
  - From Contract Commencement Date to the first **April 30** afterwards.
  - Indexed Contract Price during this period equals the **Contract Price**.

- **Subsequent Indexed Pricing Periods**:
  - Each 12‑month period from **May 1 to April 30**.
  - For each period y, the Indexed Contract Price **CP_y** is the **greater of**:
    1. Indexed Contract Price for the previous period **(CP_{y‑1})**, and
    2. CPI‑indexed value of the Contract Price, using **Percentage Escalated (PE)** and CPI ratio (**CPI_y / CPI_BD**).

- **Variables**:
  - **CP_y** – Indexed Contract Price for period y.
  - **CP** – Contract Price.
  - **CPI_BD** – CPI on the Base Date.
  - **CPI_y** – CPI for the period in question.
  - **PE** – Percentage Escalated.

- **Mechanics**:
  - Ensures CP_y **never falls** (floor at previous period's price), while allowing partial CPI‑based escalation.

**Impact**
- Indexed pricing is **functionally identical** between Version 2.0 and Version 3.1.1; there is no shift in economic indexation.

### 4.3 Appendix C – Solar Photovoltaic Schedule

**Version 2.0** – Full domestic‑content regime
- **Key definitions**:
  - *Existing Building*.
  - *Rooftop Facility*.
  - *Domestic Content Level*.
  - *Minimum Required Domestic Content Level*.

- **Domestic content methodology**:
  - Domestic Content Level is calculated by summing **Qualifying Percentages** for **Designated Activities** completed for the Facility.
  - A Designated Activity counts only if:
    - It covers the **entire** scope of the relevant components, and
    - It is supported by records **satisfactory to OPA**.

- **Supplier obligations**:
  - Obtain **written confirmations** from component suppliers and installers regarding origin and performance of Designated Activities.
  - On OPA request, deliver **within 10 Business Days** evidence that the Facility meets the Minimum Required Domestic Content Level.

- **Technology‑specific grids**:
  - Separate detailed tables for:
    - **Crystalline PV**.
    - **Thin‑film PV**.
    - **Concentrated PV**.
  - Each assigns **percentage weights** to defined Designated Activities (e.g., module assembly, cell manufacturing, inverters, mounting systems, wiring, labour) and totals 100%.

**Version 3.1.1** – Rooftop / building definitions focus
- **Existing Building**:
  - A building that was **fully constructed** as of the date the Application was first submitted, or
  - A building that OPA, in its **sole and absolute discretion**, confirms in writing will be deemed an Existing Building.

- **Rooftop Facility**:
  - A solar PV Facility integrated into or forming part of the wall, roof, cover, or other **architectural element** of a **permanent Existing Building**.
  - That element must be designed primarily to:
    - Provide **enclosure or shelter**, and
    - Not primarily to support solar power or provide sun shelter.
  - If a building (or part of it) would not reasonably have been constructed **but for the solar PV facility**, it is treated as primarily for supporting solar/sun shelter and is **not** an Existing Building unless OPA exercises its discretion.

- No domestic‑content grids or Minimum Required Domestic Content Level are described in the provided excerpt for Version 3.1.1.

**Impact**
- Under Version 2.0, domestic content is a **clearly contractual**, quantifiable obligation.
- Under Version 3.1.1 (per provided text):
  - Domestic‑content machinery is not visible; obligations may shift to program rules instead of the Contract body.
  - Rooftop classification is more tightly controlled, and OPA has broad discretion to determine whether a structure qualifies as an Existing Building. This can be pivotal for:
    - Tariff or eligibility conditions that depend on rooftop status.
    - Purpose‑built structures (e.g., carports, canopies, shade structures).

## 5. Impact Analysis and Overall Conclusions

### 5.1 Areas of Strong Continuity

Across Version 2.0 and Version 3.1.1, the following elements are **stable and aligned**:

- **Program framework**: Both contracts implement the microFIT Program under the Electricity Act, with the same two‑part Contract structure and the same set of Appendices.
- **Core eligibility and capacity rules**: 10 kW capacity cap, Renewable Fuel list, Ontario siting, prohibition on multiple facilities over 10 kW per Renewable Fuel on a Deemed Single Property, rooftop vs. non‑rooftop PV dichotomy.
- **Technical configuration**: Metering arrangements, Directly/Indirectly Connected concepts, and In Parallel configuration.
- **Environmental attributes**: Supplier assigns all environmental and regulatory attributes to OPA.
- **Indexed pricing mechanism**: Appendix B's escalation formula and period definitions.
- **LDC non‑retail Supplier regime**: Appendix E's replacement of certain Part 2 provisions for LDC Suppliers, including settlement through IESO and permanent disconnection presumptions.

For most typical microFIT projects, these similarities mean that the **overall commercial and technical framework** feels familiar moving from Version 2.0 to Version 3.1.1.

### 5.2 Material Shifts and Risk Re‑allocation

The main **substantive differences** cluster in a few key areas:

1. **Eligibility, anti‑splitting, and individual/Farmer limitations**
   - Version 3.1.1 tightens microFIT eligibility and explicitly prohibits project splitting and multiple microFIT contracts for Individuals/Farmers.
   - **Effect**: Supports program fairness and cap‑management but **narrows flexibility** for participants and increases risk that mis‑structuring or misrepresentation will justify contract remedies.

2. **Cost‑reimbursement and domestic content**
   - Version 2.0 offers explicit cost‑reimbursement (subject to prior OPA approval) for compliance costs triggered by OPA's environmental‑attribute directions, and builds domestic content into the Contract via Appendix C's grids and Section 4 representations.
   - Version 3.1.1, as summarized, **does not replicate** this level of detail in the Contract itself.
   - **Effect**: Likely shifts more control and some economic risk toward OPA and away from Suppliers, and moves domestic‑content enforcement out of the main contract text and into program documentation.

3. **General conditions and information control**
   - Version 3.1.1 requires more **active cooperation** from Suppliers: producing LDC settlement statements and correspondence, allowing direct verification with third parties, and recognizing OPA's right to mandate an authorized representative.
   - **Effect**: Increases transparency and lowers OPA's enforcement costs; imposes additional administrative and record‑keeping duties on Suppliers.

4. **Directly Connected Facilities and storage**
   - Version 3.1.1's Appendix D‑1 clearly **prohibits battery back‑up** or supply systems for Directly Connected Facilities, for the full Term.
   - **Effect**: Restricts Suppliers' ability to retrofit or integrate storage for Directly Connected microFIT projects, which may affect resilience or optimization strategies.

5. **Termination and dispute resolution (uncertain impact)**
   - Version 2.0 clearly provides:
     - A 30‑day unilateral **Supplier termination** right.
     - An optional **arbitration framework** under Ontario's Arbitration Act, with consolidation powers.
   - The absence of equivalent text in the Version 3.1.1 summaries leaves a **potentially large gap**:
     - If those clauses were removed, Suppliers in Version 3.1.1 have a weaker exit position and less certainty about dispute resolution mechanisms.
     - If they still exist but were not captured, the difference is documentary, not substantive.
   - **Effect**: Potential but unconfirmed shift of bargaining power toward OPA; should be checked against the full Version 3.1.1 Part 2 text.

### 5.3 Practical Takeaways

- **For Suppliers under Version 2.0**:
  - Benefit from:
    - A **documented ability** to terminate on 30 days' notice (subject to confirmation in the executed form).
    - Clearer contractual **cost‑reimbursement language** (where OPA directs certain actions).
    - A highly detailed domestic‑content schedule providing **certainty** about compliant component sourcing.
  - Face similar eligibility, siting, and environmental‑attribute obligations as under Version 3.1.1.

- **For Suppliers under Version 3.1.1**:
  - Operate under:
    - **Stricter eligibility** and anti‑splitting requirements.
    - Enhanced **OPA audit and information powers**.
    - Tighter **battery constraints** for Directly Connected Facilities.
    - A more formal and explicit Rooftop/Existing Building classification regime, with OPA discretion.
  - May **lack some explicit protections** present in Version 2.0 (e.g., cost‑reimbursement language, on‑its‑face termination right, explicit arbitration clause), depending on what appears in the full contract.

- **For OPA**:
  - Version 3.1.1 reinforces:
    - **Program integrity** (anti‑splitting, eligibility, information access).
    - **Control over classification** (buildings, rooftop status).
    - **Simplicity of enforcement**, by elevating representations and covenants around eligibility and facility structure.
