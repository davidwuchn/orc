# Briefing Report: YYJ 2025 Parking Management Services RFP

## Executive Summary

This document set is a Request for Proposals issued by the Victoria Airport Authority for parking management services at Victoria International Airport (YYJ), together with schedules, appendices, and a draft form of management contract. The procurement seeks a contractor to manage airport parking operations, submit a proposal under a defined process and schedule, and, if selected, enter into a long-form management contract beginning June 1, 2025.

The key institutional parties are the Victoria Airport Authority as issuer and contracting authority, Victoria International Airport as the operating site, and the prospective Proponents / eventual Contractor. The named procurement contact is David Parson, Commercial Development Officer. The draft contract also identifies Elizabeth M. Brown, President and CEO, as the VAA signatory.

The most important actions in the document are the mandatory pre-bid meeting and site tour, the March 31, 2025 proposal deadline, the expected April 24, 2025 award timing, and the planned June 1, 2025 contract commencement. The commercial structure includes a fixed management fee framework, parking rate schedules, gross-revenue handling provisions, and security/insurance requirements including an irrevocable letter of credit tied to 5% of the total fixed management fee for the initial term. The draft contract provides for a five-year initial term, with a possible two-year renewal by mutual agreement.

### Payment, reporting, and security highlights

- The Contractor will pay all Gross Revenue to the VAA less the amounts which are payable to the Contractor as set out in Part 2 of the Articles of Agreement – CONTRACT PRICE.
- The Contractor will accept the Management Fee referred to in Clause 2.1.1 of the Articles of Agreement – MANAGEMENT FEE – as full compensation for all Work provided to the VAA pursuant to this Contract, including for loss or damage arising out of the Work, unforeseen obstructions or difficulties, all risks connected with the Work, and all expenses for the account of the Contractor.
- The Contractor will pay all Gross Revenue (net of amounts payable to the Contractor under CONTRACT PRICE) for the immediately preceding month to the VAA by the fifth (5th) Business Day of each and every month of this Contract, commencing on June 6, 2025, and by the fifth (5th) Business Day of the month following the end of the Term.
- Gross Revenue payable by the Contractor to the VAA will be paid when due by Electronic Transfer of Funds (ETF) to the VAA's general bank account or by such other means as may be mutually agreed upon between the parties.
- The Contractor may deduct standard Credit Card Processing Fees from the Gross Revenue paid to the VAA by the Contractor.
- Monies not collected by the Contractor at the time such payment is due will not be accounted for as Gross Revenue until payment has been received by the Contractor.
- The successful Proponent must provide a satisfactory Irrevocable Letter of Credit equal to five percent (5%) of the total Fixed Management Fee for the Initial Term of the Contract as performance security.
- A letter of surety and undertaking is required confirming the Proponent will be able to provide the required Irrevocable Letter of Credit.
- A letter from the Proponent's insurer is required confirming that, if successful, the Proponent will be able to meet the insurance requirements specified under Schedule D – CONTRACT SECURITY AND INSURANCE.
- The successful Proponent must provide written proof of registration and good standing with Work Safe BC before the June 1, 2025 Contract commencement.
- The Proponent must provide written proof of registration and good standing with Work Safe BC, or indicate understanding and commitment to meet Work Safe BC requirements if outside British Columbia.
- The Contractor must maintain property insurance on all property for which it is legally liable, or which is installed by or on behalf of the Contractor at the Parking Facilities, including stock-in-trade (if appropriate), furniture, and fixtures, on an 'all risks' basis including sprinkler leakage (where applicable), earthquake, flood and collapse, for not less than full insurable value on a replacement cost basis with reasonable deductibles.

### Contract term highlights

- The successful Proponent will be required to provide written proof of registration and good standing with Work Safe BC before the June 1, 2025 Contract commencement.
- Parking Lot Rates in Appendix B are stated as effective March 1, 2025.
- This Contract is for an Initial Term of five (5) years beginning June 1, 2025 and ending May 31, 2030.
- The Initial Term and any renewal or extension collectively constitute the 'Term' of the Contract.
- If, at the expiration of the Initial Term, the VAA desires a renewal for a second Term of two (2) years beginning June 1, 2030, the VAA will give notice in writing of such desire to the Contractor not later than December 1, 2029 (six months before expiration of the Initial Term).
- If the Contractor desires such a renewal and the VAA has given timely notice, the VAA will grant a renewal of this Contract for a second Term of two (2) years, beginning June 1, 2030 and ending May 31, 2032 (the 'Renewal Term') on mutually agreed terms and conditions.
